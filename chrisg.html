Seed Keeper: Chris Gillis

I have been growing and saving seed for the past 10 years on the Cape starting at the Brewster Community Gardens and Harwich Community Gardens to now a garden that I share with my wife and two rescue dogs in Harwich. I was a member of the Seed Savers Exchange and sold at the Harwich Farmers Market in 2017. I focus on finding interesting varities, the resilience of seeds that work and sharing them in the Cape Cod region. All of my seed is grown without pesticides, chemical fertilizers or herbicides.

Seed belongs in the commons and in the hands of local gardeners for the good of all. Patenting of seed is obstructing biodiversity and resilience in our local food systems and should be eradicated.
What I'm currently growing and working on

Native Dry Beans, Native American Squash, Lettuce, Peppers and Tomatoes.
