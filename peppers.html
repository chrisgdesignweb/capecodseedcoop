Flame Tongue

I have been growing this pepper on Cape Cod for many years and have recently over the past two years seen some crosses that are producing larger pods. This is perfect chili plant for our region - ripens early and has short stout plants that are covered with peppers. The seed originated from Sylvia Davatz up in VT who grew it out in the cold climate for years. Flame Tongue was originally developed by seed saver Rod Trotter in Oregon. This is unstable at this point and looks to be adapting nicely to Cape Cod. This is the pepper that started my seed saving journey.

New Mexico Landrace

I have been growing this chili for a few years now on Cape Cod and it does really well in our climate which surprised me. This is an upright plant that can get to 3 feet and throws out lots of chilis with medium heat. They came my way originally from Frank Morton's Wild Garden Seed. He has been given them by Loretta Sandoval at the 2014 Organic Seed Growers Conference. It is an OSSI pledged variety which means you have the freedom to use these OSSI- Pledged seeds in any way you choose. Rosemary Pepper

Really prolific, easy to grow chili that originates from Turkey. This plant grows really well here on Cape Cod even in containers. This chilli is easy to grow, produces alot of chilis and is similar in heat to a jalepeno.
