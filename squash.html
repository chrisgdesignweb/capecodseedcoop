Sibley

Delicious large tear shaped fruits with color similar to a Blue Hubbard. The origins of the seed are obtained from an elderly woman in Van Dinam, Iowa who had grown it for more than fifty years. Hiram Sibley and Company of Rochester, New York introduced it commercially in 1887. This squash did really well for me in the hopt, hot heat of the 2018 growing season. I've only just started saving these seeds last year and will continue to grow out in 2019.

Butternut

A bunch of Butternut Squash plants had surfaced in my garden a few years back and I selected for the largest and healthiest plants. I've been growing these out and saving seed since. I have alos mixed in the Waltham Butternut Strain to this mix and last year had a spectacular year in the hot, hot summer of 2018.
